The First Star

Somewhere in the noise of an ordinary day, one star lit up brighter than the rest. I didn't know it yet, but that was the day you walked in — and the sky quietly rearranged itself around you.

CHAPTER TWO

The Constellation Forms

One star became two. Two became a shape. Every conversation, every silly argument, every 2 AM call — another point of light, quietly joining the pattern. I didn't plan this constellation. You built it, one moment at a time.

CHAPTER THREE

The Whole Sky, Rearranged

Now when I look up, I don't see stars anymore. I see us — every high, every hard day, every reason I stayed. The whole sky belongs to a story that only makes sense with you in it.
